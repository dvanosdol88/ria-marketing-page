The "Head Start" Problem

To beat the market, high-fee funds must generate 9% returns in a 7% world.

Concept A: The Zero-Sum Game

Professor Kenneth French (Dartmouth) posits that before fees, the market is a zero-sum game. If you win, someone else loses.

But after fees, active management becomes a negative-sum game. The chart on the left illustrates the "Head Start." A high-fee fund starts every year 2% behind the starting line.

"The aggregate portfolio of active investors must return the market minus fees."

Concept C: The Net Result

If Concept A is true (Market is zero-sum) and Concept B is true (Costs are controllable), then Concept C must follow: Lower costs lead to higher net returns.

This chart visualizes Morningstar's famous "Predictive Power" study. It divides funds into quintiles (buckets) from cheapest to most expensive. The trend is undeniable: as fees go up, performance goes down.
