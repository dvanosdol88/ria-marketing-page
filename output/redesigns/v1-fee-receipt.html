Three things every fee schedule
ought to tell you.

A short field guide for anyone reviewing an advisor

i.

The dollar amount

A 1% fee on a $2 million portfolio is $20,000 a year — every year — whether your account grew or shrank. Most statements only show a percentage. The dollar number ought to be in plain English on page one.

ii.

The lifetime cost

A small percentage compounds. Over thirty years at 8%, every 1% of recurring fee subtracts roughly a fifth of the wealth you would otherwise have kept. That is not opinion; it is arithmetic.

iii.

The alternative

A flat monthly retainer — $100 a month, in our case — pays the same fiduciary advice without a percentage of every additional dollar you save. The savings line up with your hard work, not the advisor's.
