Paired Front/Back EDDM Concepts

Six coordinated directions. Brand locked, copy preserved, mint fields avoided.

1. Ledger Rules

Rationale: A clean ledger-style grid makes the calculator feel factual and keeps the QR/domain path unmistakable.

Front/back fit: The front uses a structured data-report rhythm, and the back continues it with rule-led columns and a disciplined identity rail.

Print safety: High - safest for average EDDM printers.

2. Editorial Column

Rationale: A magazine-like hierarchy gives the $788,000 number more authority without adding decoration or stock imagery.

Front/back fit: The front opens like a feature spread; the back resolves into a left identity column and a strong right-side action lane.

Print safety: Medium - review ink coverage and trim tolerance.

3. Statement Border

Rationale: A print-friendly black-and-green border creates polish while using almost no tinted area.

Front/back fit: Both sides share the same framed-system language, making this the most conservative printer-friendly direction.

Print safety: High - safest for average EDDM printers.

4. White Space

Rationale: More blank space makes the mailer feel premium and lets the domain and QR breathe.

Front/back fit: The front gives the headline generous room; the back mirrors that restraint with sparse benefit blocks and a quiet QR zone.

Print safety: High - safest for average EDDM printers.

5. Proof Strip

Rationale: A narrow green proof strip creates brand continuity without becoming a mint background.

Front/back fit: The front uses the strip to anchor assumptions and the back uses it as a visual handoff from advisor identity to action.

Print safety: Medium - review ink coverage and trim tolerance.

6. Classic Direct

Rationale: A straightforward direct-mail layout maximizes readability for average EDDM printing and quick scanning.

Front/back fit: The front is a simple calculator proof; the back is a direct response layout with clear identity, benefits, QR, and domain.

Print safety: High - safest for average EDDM printers.
